Functional Requirements (FR), Non-Functional Requirements (NFR), Constraints (C) and Assumptions (A)
ID Requirement (“The system shall …”) Goal(s)
FR1 … support role-based responsibilities and approval gates across workflow stages for recurring decisions. 4,5,36
FR2 … create and maintain standardised decision packages capturing options, rationale, trade-offs, constraints, evidence, and assumptions. 7,9,27,36
FR3 … support defensible business cases for sustainability investments, incl. split incentives, by documenting value drivers and impacts. 6,9,15
FR4 … provide value-focused indicators that make benefits explicit, support adoption across teams with varying sustainability maturity. 8,39,40
FR5 … support continuous capability development by capturing, reusing lessons learned, guidelines, and decision knowledge across cycles. 11,35,39
FR6 … reduce time and effort for sustainability-related work by automating recurring preparation steps and reusing templates and data. 12,16,17
FR7 … model budgeting cycles and financial constraints, and filter or flag decision options by feasibility. 14,18
FR8 … support risk-aware option evaluation by representing uncertainty ranges, sensitivities, and investment risk indicators. 15,27
FR9 … maintain a consolidated, asset-centric single source of truth, including a coherent, traceable building record with components, materials, condition, and intervention history. 19,20,
23,41
FR10 … maintain an up-to-date as-is building representation by reconciling plans or models with operational changes over time. 42,43,46
FR11 … ingest, index, search, and link legacy and unstructured documentation to assets, components, and decision levels for reuse. 20,21,41
FR12 … implement completeness, timeliness, plausibility, and conflict checks and expose confidence indicators for decision-relevant data. 22,27
FR13 … support decision-making with partial or access-restricted data by using aggregated or proxy values and marking their limitations. 19,25
FR14 … identify missing decision inputs and support transparent external sourcing by tracking requests, received data, and provenance. 24,28
FR15 … enable technical interoperability via standardised interfaces and integrate external tool outputs and consultant deliverables into internal workflows. 13,29,
30,34,37
FR16 … provide advanced scenario analysis and simulation capabilities to compare options across recurring decision cycles using consistent logic and outputs across portfolio, asset, and building levels. 18,31,
32,44,47
NFR1 … provide end-to-end audit trails for decisions, including inputs, approvals, rationale, and evidence. 7,36
NFR2 … ensure reproducibility by versioning decision-relevant data, rules/standards, and explicit assumptions. 27
NFR3 … minimise user effort and process overhead and remain usable under time and staffing constraints. 12,17
NFR4 … remain decision-useful under heterogeneous data baselines across organisations and assets. 19,25
NFR5 … enforce role-based access control and privacy safeguards for sensitive datasets and stakeholder boundaries. 5,25
NFR6 … avoid creating a new silo by supporting interoperable data exchange and integration patterns across the existing tool landscape. 29,30
NFR7 … provide maintainable integrations through stable connectors, controlled versioning, and low integration maintenance overhead. 30,33
NFR8 … ensure consistent calculations and comparable outputs across assets and cycles to enable fair option comparison. 31,36
NFR9 … make trade-offs, constraints, and uncertainty explicit in outputs so results are explainable and defensible. 9,15,22
NFR10 … deliver decision-relevant outputs within time limits suitable for recurring planning and operational cycles. 12,16
C1 Data availability and quality vary across organisations and assets so the solution must accommodate heterogeneous data baselines (e.g., BIM).
C2 Decision-relevant information is often available in unstructured or physical formats such as paper documents and requires digitisation effort.
C3 Tenant-related and consumption data are often unavailable or only partially accessible due to privacy constraints.
C4 Asset-specific budgets constrain sustainability action by limiting measures that are feasible regardless of their sustainability impact.
A1 External consultants will remain necessary, so the solution must integrate their deliverables rather than assume full in-house capability.
A2 Sustainability maturity varies across organisations and teams, so the solution must make its value visible even under passive adoption.
